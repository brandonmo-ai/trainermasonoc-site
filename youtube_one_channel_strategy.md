## Verified facts (from official YouTube/FTC)

- Expanded YPP (in eligible countries including the U.S.) allows entry at **500 subs + 3 uploads/90 days + either 3,000 watch hours/12 months or 3 million Shorts views/90 days** for fan-funding + some shopping features. Ad revenue share access remains at **1,000 + 4,000 watch hours or 10 million Shorts views**.  
  Source: YouTube Help (expanded YPP).  
  https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/13429240

- YPP also requires channel policy compliance, no active strikes, 2-step verification, and linked AdSense.  
  Source: YouTube Help (YPP overview).  
  https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/72851

- Channels accepted into YPP can lose monetization if inactive for 6+ months (no upload or Posts activity).  
  Source: YouTube Help (YPP overview + expanded YPP).  
  https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/72851  
  https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/13429240

- Fan-funding features (memberships, Supers, etc.) require YPP status, age 18+, location availability, and compliance with commerce/monetization policies.  
  Source: YouTube Help (Commerce products).  
  https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/13195878

- YouTube Shopping affiliate program is available to eligible creators in specified countries including the U.S.; commissions and attribution windows are set by participating sellers; payout can take **60–120 days** after purchase (returns can reverse commissions).  
  Source: YouTube Help (Shopping affiliate overview).  
  https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/13376398

- YouTube requires paid promotion declaration (“paid promotion” setting) for relevant videos; creators/brands are responsible for legal disclosures in their jurisdiction.  
  Source: YouTube Help (paid promotions).  
  https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/154235

  Source: YouTube channel monetization policies.  
  https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/1311392

## 5) Monetization ladder (single-channel)

**Important:** no fabricated rates; where uncertain, marked unknown.

## Before 500 subscribers
- Primary focus: audience problem-solution proof, email capture, and authority.  
- Monetize now:  
  - 1:1 starter audit calls (limited spots).  
  - Simple paid 4-week challenge (Google Doc + check-ins).  
  - External affiliate links (where allowed + disclosed clearly).  
- Ads from YPP: **not available yet**.

## Around 500 subscribers (if expanded YPP thresholds met)
- Apply for expanded YPP to unlock fan-funding/shopping pathways where eligible.  
- Potential unlocks: channel memberships, Supers, shopping features (eligibility + country constraints apply).  
- Use low-ticket “execution products” first: templates, habit trackers, training blocks.

## Around 1,000 subscribers (if higher YPP thresholds met)
- Ads/Premium revenue sharing becomes available when 1,000 + 4,000 watch hours or 10M Shorts views criteria are met.  
- Add first small recurring membership tier with clear deliverables (weekly Q&A, templates, private stream notes).

## Around 10,000 subscribers
- Structured brand-deal packages (integrated + Shorts + live mention + usage terms).  
- Group coaching cohorts.  
- First flagship digital product (8–12 week program).  
- Build recurring community operations (monthly programming calendar).

## Brand deals strategy
- Offer category fit only (fitness apparel, food prep, recovery, training tech).  
- Start with performance + audience-fit pitch deck, not vanity metrics.  
- Mandatory dual disclosure: YouTube paid promotion tool + FTC-clear language.

## Affiliate strategy
- Start with products you already use on camera.  
- Build “stack pages” by use-case (home gym basics, lean bulk foods, recovery).  
- Track by content pillar and scenario (unknown initial CVR).

## Own products path
1. Notion/Google Sheet tracker pack  
2. 4-week execution challenge  
3. Group coaching cohort  
4. Full transformation program/course  
5. Hybrid membership + app layer

## Membership/community
- Tier 1: weekly accountability thread + checklist.  
- Tier 2: monthly live review + program updates.  
- Tier 3 (later): program library + office hours.

## Coaching path
- Begin with limited diagnostic calls.  
- Move to cohort-based small groups for time efficiency.  
- Reserve 1:1 premium only after demand exceeds time.

## Course path
- Do **not** start with a large course.  
- Validate via paid challenge + repeated Q&A objections first.  
- Then ship a short, outcome-focused program.

## Merch/app/brand path
- Merch only after audience identity is strong (slogans from community language).  
- App later only when recurring programming process is stable and retention is proven.

## 8) What to avoid (critical)

1. **Reused / inauthentic mass-produced content**: can hurt monetization eligibility and channel trust.  
2. **Factory-style AI spam**: low authenticity increases platform and audience risk.  
3. **Misleading health claims**: avoid “guaranteed cure,” dangerous substance claims, and guidance contradicting health authorities.  
4. **Bad sponsorship disclosures**: always mark paid promotion + clear FTC-compliant wording.  
5. **Channel drift**: keep every topic tied to “young men’s physique + confidence systems.”  
6. **Weak offers**: avoid vague “mentorship”; sell specific outcomes, timelines, and deliverables.
